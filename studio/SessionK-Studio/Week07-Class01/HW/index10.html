FAG

Artist: Han
Studio: Stuy Dorm
Cost: Free
Bodypart: Bottom of the foot
Year: 2018

Description
Most people get tattoos for themselves even if in honor of another person or thing. It is rare a tattoo fulfills a practical purpose or a means of completing a college project. I had a professor give us a project where we were to find an artist that inspired us and create a piece inspired by the artist’s work. Through some research, I came across Catherine Opie and fell in love with her photography but more importantly, her subjects and concepts. Throughout much of her work, the theme of queerness dominates the forefront especially the concept of butch lesbians. I was inspired by her piece Dyke (1993) to reflect on my own queer experience and the impact of language. Opie’s aim with this piece was to take away the power given to words such as “dyke” and how a tattoo is one of the most permanent and extreme ways to label one’s self in this manner. I consider myself highly lucky and am deeply grateful for the people I have in my life and how they welcomed my coming out and queer relationships. I am in a very privileged position and have always felt empowered by the word “fag” and have never let it bother me. However, there was a boy from my high school who commented “faggy” on a post of me and a girl I was dating at the time. It didn’t bother me too much but it made me realize there is still a cultural realm where people are still trying to bring queer people down. I decided to get “Fag” tattooed on my foot as a representation of a label I am not ashamed of. My good friend did it with a stick n’ poke method and it was a very special experience being surrounded by my friends who love and accept me and willing to help me no matter what. I don’t know if I would have gotten the tattoo without having gotten the assignment but none the less it is one of my most personal tattoos.
